Is Now a Good Time to Buy on the Emerald Coast?

By Jessica Mackrael · Market Updates

It's the question every buyer asks first, and the honest answer is: it depends less on the calendar than on your own timeline and how prepared you are to move when the right home appears.

A few things worth knowing about how this market actually behaves:

  • Inventory shifts by season. Late winter and early spring typically bring more new listings across Niceville, Destin, and 30A — more choices, but more competition too.
  • Well-priced homes still move quickly. Homes that are prepared and priced correctly for their neighborhood routinely go under contract in days, not weeks.
  • Financing changes the math. Rate environments shift throughout the year — getting pre-approved early tells you your real budget before you fall for a home that's out of range.
  • Waterfront and 30A carry their own rhythm. Luxury and vacation-driven segments respond more to buyer demand than to the broader market calendar.

The short version: the best time to buy is when you're financially ready and working with someone who knows which listings are worth moving fast on. That's the conversation worth having before you start scrolling listings.

Selling

Preparing Your Home to Sell This Season

By Jessica Mackrael · Market Updates

First impressions decide most of a home's outcome before a buyer ever walks through the door. A little preparation goes a long way toward a faster sale and a stronger price.

  • Start outside. Buyers judge curb appeal in seconds — fresh mulch, a clean walkway, and a tidy entry set the tone before anything else.
  • Declutter before you stage. Clear counters, closets, and shelves so buyers can picture their own life in the space, not yours.
  • Fix the small stuff. Leaky faucets, scuffed walls, and burnt-out bulbs are cheap fixes that otherwise plant doubt about bigger issues.
  • Price it to the current market, not last year's. The right price on day one attracts serious buyers immediately — overpricing usually costs more in the long run than it gains.
  • Photograph it right. On the Emerald Coast, listing photos are doing most of the selling before a buyer ever schedules a showing.

Every home and every neighborhood is a little different — a walkthrough with a clear plan for pricing, prep, and marketing is the fastest way to know exactly where you stand.

A Military Family's Guide to PCS Home Buying on the Emerald Coast

By Jessica Mackrael, MRP · Market Updates

PCS orders come with a deadline attached, which makes home buying near Eglin Air Force Base and Hurlburt Field a different process than a typical purchase. Here's how to make it work on a military timeline.

  • Start before you're local. Video walkthroughs and virtual tours let you shortlist homes and make real decisions before your household goods even ship.
  • Know your VA loan advantage. VA financing often means no down payment and no PMI — but timelines and appraisal requirements move differently than conventional loans, so plan around them early.
  • Work backward from report date. A good closing plan is built around your report date, not the other way around — with buffer for the unexpected.
  • Consider commute and base access. Neighborhoods around Niceville, Bluewater Bay, and Fort Walton Beach each offer a different trade-off between commute time, schools, and price.
  • Lean on someone who's done this before. An agent fluent in PCS timelines can coordinate inspections, financing, and closing dates around orders — remotely, if needed.

As a Certified Military Relocation Professional based minutes from Eglin AFB, this is the move Jessica helps families make most often — often before they've even landed in Florida.
